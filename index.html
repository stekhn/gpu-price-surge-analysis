The GPU Price Surge

Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies have climbed in value over the past year, thanks to increased demand from a range of investors. As the price of cryptocurrencies has increased, graphics card prices have skyrocketed.

The price surge is hurting gamers and everyone looking to buy a graphics cards for reasons other then cryptocurreny mining. This analysis shows how the increasing value for ten different cryptocurrencies is driving the price for GPUs on the German online marketplaces.

GPUs Cryptocurrencies

Only half a year ago, the Nvidia Geforce GTX 1070 was available for about €410, now most GTX 1070 models are sold close to €600. Cryptocurrency miners are buying lots of mid- to high-end graphics cards to keep their operations profitable in a very competitive market. The price for the average GPU was already above average in summer, but with the sudden spike in Bitcoin value – and public interest – before Christmas, we are in the middle of a second GPU price surge.

The price surge is fueled by an increasing demand for alternative cryptocurrencies, like Ethereum and Monero. It’s a problem that’s affecting pricing worldwide, not just in the Germny, and it seems like card vendors can’t keep up with the demand. Places that are attractive to cryptocurrencies miners, because electricity is cheap, might see a even bigger increase in GPU retail prices.

A sudden and continuing devaluation of cryptocurrencies could flood the second hand market with cheap, used graphic cards from former mining rigs. In this case we could put those GPUs to use, run PC games at 4K resolution, train neural networks or crack our neighbors’ WiFi passwords.

Methodology

The list of graphic cards includes popular, mid- to high-end consumer models. The prices have been retrieved from the German price comparison service Idealo.

  • GeForce GTX 1080 Ti: GigaByte, MSI, Zotac
  • GeForce GTX 1080: GigaByte, Zotac, MSI
  • GeForce GTX 1070 Ti: GigaByte, MSI, Zotac
  • GeForce GTX 1070: GigaByte, MSI, Zotac
  • Radeon RX 580: GigaByte, MSI, Sapphire
  • Radeon RX 570: Sapphire, Powercolor, GigaByte

The Radeon RX Vega56, Vega64 and Geforce GTX TITAN X have been excluded from the analysis, because they are expensive and their prices are very volatile due to retail stock shortages. The old Radeon RX 480 – another popular choice – was excluded because it's only available on the second-hand market.

The selection of cryptocurrencies is based on what I consider “important currencies”. The price history was retrieved from the CryptoCompare.
